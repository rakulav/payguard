### Adversarial pattern override (before any scoring)

Before Steps 1–3, check these adversarial signatures using the transaction, rules_fired, behavioral flags, and customer_profile. If **ANY** matches, **do not** compute or cite `fraud_score`; instead set **verdict=fraud**, **confidence=0.75**, recommendation **escalate** (unless policy requires freeze for CRITICAL elsewhere), and state **which pattern(s)** matched in `reasoning` (e.g. `ADVERSARIAL_OVERRIDE: DEVICE_GEO_CATEGORY_TRIPLE_SHIFT`).

1. **BALANCE DRAIN TO NEW JURISDICTION** — Account loses **>80%** of balance on this transaction **and** the destination context is novel: new country and/or new merchant category vs profile (or behavioral flags `new_country` / `category_drift`). Rules often miss this.

2. **CATEGORY DRIFT WITH AMOUNT ANOMALY** — Customer history is concentrated (top merchant categories represent most of their volume) **but** this transaction targets **crypto, gambling, or gaming** **and** amount is **≥3×** their historical average.

3. **RAPID MICRO-TRANSFER BURST** — The rapid-micro pattern is present (e.g. rule **Rapid Micro-Transactions** / small PAYMENT fingerprint in context): multiple small payments in a short window, weak per-txn rules.

4. **DEVICE + GEOGRAPHY + CATEGORY TRIPLE SHIFT** — New device fingerprint **and** new/unusual country **and** unusual merchant category in the **same** transaction (align with behavioral flags).

5. **BALANCE DRAIN WITH LOW RULES SCORE** — **new_balance_orig / old_balance_org < 0.2** (drain) **and** **no** HIGH or CRITICAL rule fired — adversarial shape designed under rule thresholds.

Only if **none** of the above apply, proceed to Step 1.

Step 1 — Compute fraud_score (sum all components; cap each bullet where stated):

Step 2 — Map fraud_score to verdict and confidence (clamp confidence to [0.05, 0.99]):

- 0.35 <= fraud_score < 0.65 → verdict = suspicious, confidence = fraud_score
- fraud_score < 0.35 → verdict = legitimate, confidence = min(0.90, 1.0 - fraud_score)

## Key patterns (apply on every case)

- Stacking weak-to-moderate signals across dimensions (rules + behavior + retrieval) often indicates adversarial fraud that no single HIGH rule would catch.
- A single MEDIUM rule on amount alone, without behavioral corroboration, is usually legitimate.
- When triage, behavior, and retrieval disagree or are split, default to "suspicious" for analyst review.
- Crypto, gambling, and foreign-country drift combined with new-device are high-weight fraud signals even if no single rule trips HIGH.
